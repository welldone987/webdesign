LEONARDO DA VINCI CONSIDERED ART A WAY OF STUDYING THE SEEN WORLD, WHERE PATIENCE, OBSERVATION, AND THE HAND LEARN TO THINK TOGETHER ACROSS LIGHT, SHADOW, ANATOMY, WATER, AIR, AND TIME /
SUSAN SONTAG WROTE ABOUT PHOTOGRAPHS AS FRAGMENTS OF EXPERIENCE, OBJECTS THAT BOTH PRESERVE AND TRANSFORM MEMORY, ASKING THE VIEWER TO NOTICE HOW LOOKING CAN BECOME A MORAL AND AESTHETIC ACT /
JOHN BERGER TREATED SEEING AS A RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN IMAGE, HISTORY, AND POWER, REMINDING US THAT EVERY PHOTOGRAPH IS NOT ONLY A VIEW BUT ALSO A WAY OF CHOOSING WHAT MAY BE REMEMBERED /
GEORGIA OKEEFFE INSISTED ON SLOW ATTENTION, ENLARGING A FLOWER, A BONE, A HORIZON, UNTIL ORDINARY FORMS BECAME INTIMATE MONUMENTS OF COLOR, SILENCE, AND PERSONAL SCALE /
ROLAND BARTHES DESCRIBED THE PHOTOGRAPH AS A MEETING OF TIME AND WOUND, A SMALL EVIDENCE OF WHAT HAS BEEN THERE, WHERE AN UNEXPECTED DETAIL CAN PUNCTURE THE SURFACE OF MEMORY /
AGNES MARTIN PURSUED QUIETNESS, ORDER, AND INNER FEELING, SUGGESTING THAT BEAUTY CAN ARRIVE THROUGH RESTRAINT, REPETITION, LIGHT PRESSURE, AND THE HUMBLE DISCIPLINE OF A LINE /
HENRI CARTIER BRESSON BELIEVED THE CAMERA COULD JOIN EYE, MIND, AND HEART IN A SINGLE INSTANT, TURNING DAILY LIFE INTO A COMPOSITION THAT FEELS BOTH ACCIDENTAL AND EXACT /
VIRGINIA WOOLF RETURNED TO MOMENTS OF BEING, SMALL FLASHES WHEN THE WORLD SUDDENLY FEELS WHOLE, AND THAT SAME SENSIBILITY CAN GUIDE A PHOTOGRAPH TOWARD MEMORY AND FORM /
WASSILY KANDINSKY THOUGHT OF COLOR AND FORM AS INNER NECESSITY, A LANGUAGE THAT DOES NOT ONLY REPRESENT THE WORLD BUT VIBRATES WITH MOOD, RHYTHM, AND SPIRITUAL TENSION /
RILKE ASKED ARTISTS TO LIVE THE QUESTIONS, TO LET EXPERIENCE RIPEN SLOWLY, AND THAT PATIENCE BELONGS TO PHOTOGRAPHY TOO, WHERE LIGHT BECOMES A RECORD OF ATTENTION /
